Prathamesh Saraf

I am a second-year Robotics Graduate Student at UC San Diego, actively looking for full-time opportunities starting January 2024.

Currently, I am working on the dynamics and controls of a biped robot at N Robotics. During my summer internship at Entegris Inc., I am building the electrical-software side of control systems used in semiconductor manufacturing. At UCSD, I explored Multi-Agent Co-operative Control, Non-Linear Control, Convex Optimization, Sensing and Estimation for Robotics, and Linear Algebra through projects and coursework.

Prior to this, I was a Research Assistant under Prof. Shishir Kolathaya in Stoch Lab, Indian Institute of Science, developing the well-known Stoch quadruped robot. I was fortunate enough to work with Prof. Guillaume Sartoretti at the National University of Singapore on gradient based torque control of the hexapod robot Daisy by Hebi Robotics.

In my undergraduate years, I had several opportunities to closely work with veterans in robotics and controls like Prof. Alivelu Manga Parimi, Prof. R.N. Ponnalagu, and Prof. Arshad Javed. Throughout the process, I've enhanced my grasp of dynamic modeling, system design, integration, optimal control algorithms, and reinforcement learning.

In my leisure time, you will usually find me painting, dancing or gyming. Apart from this, I enjoy traveling and hiking. Cooking turns out to be my stressbuster.
